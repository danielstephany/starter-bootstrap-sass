Paving the Way for New Therapies

The horizon of stem cell science is wide open. Research investigating the role of newborn stem cells in a number of therapies is currently underway. These trials represent a new, exciting age in cord blood and tissue research:

  • Alzheimer's disease

    Alzheimer’s disease is the most prevalent progressive form of age-related dementia, and is likely to only affect more and more people. The dementia patient population expected to increase to 65.7 million by 2030 and 115.4 million by 2050.

    Studies using umbilical cord stem cells suggest they may play a role in the fight against Alzheimer’s disease.

    Close
  • Diabetes

    Type I and II diabetes result in the body’s inability to regulate blood sugar (glucose), ultimately leading to poor blood circulation, heart disease, stroke, infection, kidney failure, decreased quality of life, and often premature death.

    Ongoing studies suggest that cord blood stem cell therapy can help patients with type 1 diabetes achieve better metabolic control. Patients suffering from type 2 diabetes also benefited from the same treatment.

    Close
  • Liver Disease

    Cirrhosis is a late stage of scarring (fibrosis) of the liver caused by many forms of liver diseases and conditions, such as hepatitis and chronic alcoholism.

    A clinical trial is currently looking at the ability of cord blood cells to help patients with liver cirrhosis.

    Close
  • Acute Lung Injury

    Acute Lung Injury occurs when lung tissue becomes damaged and scarred

    Clinical trials are currently in progress to investigate the use of umbilical cord stem cells in lung injury and pulmonary fibrosis.

    Close
  • Stroke

    A stroke occurs when the blood supply to part of your brain is interrupted or severely reduced, depriving brain tissue of oxygen and nutrients.

    Ongoing studies are investigating the use of cord blood in stroke treatment.

    Close
  • Peripheral Artery Disease.

    Peripheral artery disease (also called peripheral arterial disease) is a common circulatory problem in which narrowed arteries reduce blood flow to your limbs.

    An ongoing clinical trial is studying the safety and efficacy of umbilical cord stem cell for secondary peripheral arterial disease. [28] Research is also being performed on the use of stem cells for treating ischaemic limbs.

    Close
  • Heart Repair

    After a stroke, the muscles in the heart become progressively weaker and often require repair.

    Recent studies are investigating the use of umbilical cord blood for the purpose of cardiac repair of congenital heart disease.

    Close
  • Wound Healing

    Wounds can often go through several stages before they are healed.

    A recent study has been testing the healing effects of cord blood on wounds.

    Close
  • Spinal Cord Injury

    Spinal cord injury is damage to any part of the spinal cord and can cause permanent changes in strength, sensation and other body functions.

    Ongoing clinical trials are investigating the potential use of cord blood cells in the treatment of spinal cord injury.

    Close
  • Cerebral Palsy

    Cerebral palsy, the leading cause of disability in early childhood, is a group of disorders that often leads to abnormal movement and posture, cognitive impairments, and lifelong functional deficits.

    A recent study shows that umbilical cord blood treatment can improve motor and cognitive function in children with cerebral palsy undergoing active rehabilitation, and create structural and metabolic changes in the brain.
